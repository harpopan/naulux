*Your brain works completely differently when you put pen to paper compared to when you tap away on a keyboard.** According to groundbreaking research by Norwegian neuroscientist Audrey van der Meer, handwriting forces your brain to actively engage, process information, and think deeply. While typing allows your mind to coast on autopilot, the physical act of writing by hand activates widespread brain connectivity essential for learning and memory encoding.
In a digital-first world, we are rapidly losing this tactile connection to our thoughts. Typing might be faster, but speed comes at the cost of deep cognitive engagement. When you type, you are simply pressing identical keys; when you write, every letter requires a unique, precise motor movement that leaves a distinct neural footprint.
This isn't just about old-school nostalgia—it’s about optimal brain performance. If you want to retain information better, boost your creativity, and truly process what you are learning, it is time to bring back the notebook. The next time you need to brainstorm, study, or plan, put the laptop away and grab a pen. Give your brain the workout it deserves.
