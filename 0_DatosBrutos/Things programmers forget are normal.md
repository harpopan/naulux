Things programmers forget are normal. ❤️ | Part 11.

Often I see social media painting a wrong picture about programming and only showing "the good parts". And I may have my share in that too although that never is my intention, I can understand it may be perceived like that some times.

So here a friendly reminder to not let social media fool you. 🫶

✨ It's normal using trial and error because you forgot basic maths.
✨ It's normal to feel like an impostor even after years of experience. I know many senior devs who have this feeling (including myself).
✨ It's normal to feel like AI is making you more productive but less creative.
✨ It's normal to worrying you'll become just a copy-paste engineer in an AI world.. The future is always unknown.
✨ It's normal to worry you are falling behind. The industry still moves so fast and it never ends.
✨ It's normal to sit in a meeting and not understanding half the acronyms used. But feel free to ask what they mean if possible. We can't remember them all (and some are just trying to be interesting 😉)
✨ It's normal have a standing desk and barely use it to stand. Often going for a short walk feels better.
✨ It's normal to need a break to focus. You also deserve it.
✨ It's normal to code for 8 hours but only producing 10 lines of code. Most of the work is done by thinking and trial and error.
✨ It's normal may feel guilty for not coding in your spare time. It's the passion that drives us and it's the fear of missing out that scares everyone. (But don't worry, it's okay to not code in your spare time)
✨ It's normal to have more ideas than time. But it's better having more than less. 🙌

🧠 Reminder:
You’re not the only one dealing with these. Every dev, no matter their experience, hits these walls or has these feelings. It's normal! You got this! 🔥

Happy coding everyone! 👨‍💻
